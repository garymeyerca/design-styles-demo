FAQ

A design movement that strips the web back to its raw materials — system fonts or plain monospace, default-looking controls, visible structure, no decoration for decoration's sake. It rejects polish, gradients and soft comfort in favour of bluntness and honesty.

Often, yes — high contrast, real focus rings and big hit targets are core to the look. This page keeps semantic HTML, ARIA on every custom control, and respects reduced-motion. Brutal does not mean broken.

SEC.03

The Manifesto

Three rules. No exceptions. The structure is the style.

Rule 01

Sharp corners only

Zero border-radius. Every box is a hard 90-degree rectangle. Comfort is a distraction — give the eye honest edges.

Rule 02

Hard offset shadows

No blur, ever. A shadow is a solid black duplicate, offset by 8px. Press a button and it slams down — the shadow collapses.

Rule 03

One loud accent

Monospace type, near-black ink, and exactly one jarring colour. Used sparingly so it stays jarring. Restraint through excess.
