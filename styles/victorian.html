Matters of Enquiry

An aesthetic of abundant ornament — damask patterning, gilt framing, fleur-de-lis flourishes and a deep, jewel-toned palette. Nothing is left plain where it might instead be embellished.

To the Victorian sensibility, decoration is a virtue — a sign of craft, prosperity and care. Yet every flourish here is arranged with order, so the parlour reads as composed rather than cluttered.

Wherever opulence and heritage are to be conveyed — boutiques, fine publications, theatres and emporia that wish their interface to feel like a richly appointed drawing room.

Three Tenets of the Parlour

Victorian design is ornament governed by order.

Tenet I

Abundant Ornament

Damask wallpapers, gilt frames and flourishes at every corner. The eye should never want for detail to admire.

Tenet II

Jewel-Toned Riches

Deep burgundy and bottle green, lit by gilt gold and ivory. The palette of a candlelit drawing room after dusk.

Tenet III

Order Within

Symmetry and framing keep the abundance composed — every flourish set deliberately, never strewn about.
