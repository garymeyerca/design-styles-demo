Catechism

A style that revives the restraint of Greek and Roman antiquity — measured proportion, fluted columns, laurel ornament, antique gold, and a strict axis of bilateral symmetry that lends every screen a museum-like calm.

It is — restraint serves legibility. Ink-dark text on a marble ground gives generous contrast, while engraved capitals, clear focus rings and considered labels keep every instrument operable.

Wherever dignity is desired — cultural archives, reading rooms, journals and institutions — interfaces meant to feel composed, permanent, and unhurried rather than fashionable.

Three Canons, One Order

Neoclassicism is composure — proportion, symmetry, and ornament held in measure.

I

Bilateral Symmetry

Every composition pivots on a single central axis. What stands to the left is answered, in measure, upon the right.

II

Measured Proportion

Fluted columns, thin double rules and the golden mean govern spacing — order felt, never announced.

III

Restrained Ornament

Laurel, medallion and pediment appear sparingly. Antique gold is the single accent — dignity over display.
