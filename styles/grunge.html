// faq // letters to the editor

Late-90s zine aesthetic dragged onto the web. Think David Carson’s Ray Gun magazine, Cobain’s liner-note collages, photocopied flyers stapled to telephone poles. Imperfect alignment is the point. Stains are the point. Type that looks like it was warped through a fax machine is the point.

Sort of. But ugly with intent. The mess is choreographed — every torn edge, every off-kilter rotation, every smudge is placed. Underneath it the layout still has hierarchy, contrast, and structure. The dirt is texture, not chaos.

Music labels, indie zines, hardcore festival sites, brewery cans, skate brands, anything that wants to feel hand-made and anti-corporate. Probably not great for your bank app. Definitely great for the “about” page of a band that doesn’t want to look like every other band.

// manifesto

Three rules. Break two.

Grunge isn’t the absence of rules — it’s the rules half-erased, taped back together, and re-photocopied until they bleed.

// rule 01

Texture is content

Grain, noise, smudge, scratch — they aren’t decoration on top of the design. They are the design. Strip them off and you’ve got a wireframe.

// rule 02

Mix the type

Two faces minimum, three is better. A typewriter, a marker, a stencil. Ransom-note logic — borrowed letters held together with tape.

// rule 03

Tilt everything

Nothing sits at 0°. Half a degree of rotation reads as accidental — five degrees reads as defiant. Pick your angle and commit.
