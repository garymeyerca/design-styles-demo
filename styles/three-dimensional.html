Neumorphism is subtle and monochrome — one tone, two soft shadows. Glassmorphism is flat and translucent. Three-Dimensional is loud: bold colour, real tilt, floating geometric primitives, and shadows you can read from across the room.

Three rules of depth

Lighting, shadow, perspective. The whole style follows from these three.

Rule 01

Gradient shading

Every surface gets a top-to-bottom or radial gradient that fakes a single light source. Flat fills are forbidden — even buttons earn a highlight and a shadow band.

Rule 02

Layered cast shadows

Two or three stacked drop-shadows give each element real elevation. Bigger lifts => longer, softer, more offset shadows pooling below.

Rule 03

Live perspective

Cards tilt on the X-axis, knobs pitch toward you, orbs rotate in actual 3D space. Nothing sits flat against the screen — everything has an orientation.
