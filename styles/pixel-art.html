FAQ Quest Log

An interface drawn like a 16-bit console game: a strict limited palette, hard square edges, visible chunky pixels, dithered shading and stepped hard shadows instead of blur.

Old hardware could only show a handful of colours per tile. Keeping the palette tiny forces a cohesive look and makes every sprite read instantly — constraint as a feature.

Games, retro tools, music players and playful product pages — anywhere a nostalgic, grid-based, unmistakably-digital character is the point.

> THE PIXEL RULESET

Three laws that keep every sprite sharp and on-grid.

Rule 01

STRICT PALETTE

About eight colours, chosen once and reused everywhere. No gradients beyond hard dithered steps — cohesion through limitation.

Rule 02

HARD EDGES

Zero rounding, zero blur. Depth is faked with stepped, hard-offset shadows — every shape lands cleanly on the pixel grid.

Rule 03

STEPPED MOTION

Animation jumps frame-to-frame with steps() timing — choppy on purpose, just like a real low-framerate sprite.
