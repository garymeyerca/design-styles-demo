Letters & Questions

A decorating style that finds beauty in age and wear — whitewashed wood, faded florals, soft cottage hues and antiques that look gently loved rather than freshly bought.

Imperfection reads as history. A scuffed frame or faded pattern suggests a thing has been kept and treasured — and that quiet sense of belonging is what makes a room feel like home.

Gentle, unhurried spaces — a sunlit parlour, a keepsake diary, a little shop of pretty things — anywhere romance and softness are welcome over slickness and shine.

Three gentle truths

Shabby chic is patience — beauty allowed to soften with time

Truth the First

Wear is welcome

A scuff, a fade, a frayed edge — each one a small story. Nothing here pretends to be brand new, and that is precisely the charm.

Truth the Second

Faded, never loud

Every colour is gently aged — dusty rose, sage and powder blue, all softened as though sun-bleached on a windowsill for years.

Truth the Third

Florals & flourishes

Roses, scripts and ornate frames adorn without crowding — pretty details that whisper romance rather than shout for notice.
