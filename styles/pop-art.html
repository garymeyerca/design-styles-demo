A UI style lifted straight from 1960s comics and Pop Art — Ben-Day halftone dots, fat black ink outlines, primary comic colours and hard-offset shadows. Lichtenstein and Warhol, but clickable.

Surprisingly yes — thick outlines and high-contrast primary colours give bold, obvious affordances. This page adds dashed focus rings, ARIA labels and honours reduced-motion to keep it operable.

Three rules, one BANG

Pop Art is maximalism — loud, graphic, and proudly printed.

Rule 01

Ink Everything

Every shape gets a fat 3-4px black outline, like a comic-book panel. No element floats without its bold ink border.

Rule 02

Halftone & Hue

Ben-Day dots for shading, primary comic colours for fills — red, yellow, blue, black and white. No muddy gradients.

Rule 03

Hard Shadows

Shadows are solid black, sharp and offset — never blurred. Press a button and it slams down with a satisfying CLACK.
