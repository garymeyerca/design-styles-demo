Studio notes

The drawings aren't decoration — they share the page with the controls and react to them. A character peers down at the dial; a sun beams above the brightness slider; a cat sleeps in the album art. The UI stays crisp; the world around it is hand-made.

Editorial illustration leans on a tight palette — five colours, used confidently. Marigold for warmth, teal for depth, coral for accents, leaf for life, and a near-black forest ink for every line. Nothing else allowed inside the lines.

Editorial, not whimsical. Characters have weight; lines have confidence; type does most of the talking. We borrow the calm of The New Yorker's spot illustrations, not the sparkle of a sticker pack.

Three rules from the studio

Editorial illustration as UI keeps three principles close to the chest.

01

Hand over hand

Every illustration is drawn for this page, in this palette, at this moment. No stock people, no clipart. The wobble of a human line is the whole point.

02

Drawings do work

The hero illustration isn't a header — it sets the mood. The sun over the slider isn't decoration — it labels brightness. Drawings carry meaning, not just charm.

03

Crisp where it counts

The controls stay sharp and functional. Imperfection lives in the margins, in the plants, in the characters — never in the targets you actually need to press.
