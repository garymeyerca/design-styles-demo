FAQ

A hybrid aesthetic blending Japanese wabi-sabi — the beauty of imperfection and restraint — with Scandinavian hygge: warmth, function, and comfort. Both traditions prize natural materials, muted palettes and uncluttered space.

Very. Restraint improves clarity — fewer competing signals means the one accent and the hairline structure carry meaning. Generous spacing and clear focus rings keep every control legible and easy to operate.

The Principles

Three quiet rules, one feeling

Japandi is the discipline of subtraction — keep only what is honest and necessary.

01

Restraint over decoration

One muted accent, hairline borders, modest type. Every flourish removed until only the essential structure remains. Less, but better.

02

Space as material

Negative space — ma in Japanese — is treated as a component in its own right. Generous margins let each element breathe and be understood.

03

Warmth and honesty

Natural off-whites, wood tones and warm greys. Soft, slow transitions. Materials shown plainly, with quiet imperfection welcomed.
