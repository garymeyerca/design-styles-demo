FAQ

A 1945–1965 design movement — clean geometric forms, organic curves, honest materials and a warm optimistic palette, championed by Eames, Saarinen and Scandinavian designers.

Post-war fascination with science and space gave us starbursts, boomerangs and amoeba shapes — playful graphic shorthand for a confident, forward-looking era.

Restraint. Function leads form, every line earns its place, and the warm palette is timeless — good design, as the era promised, was built to last.

Three rules, one era

Mid-Century Modern is optimism made geometric.

01

Warm retro palette

Mustard, teal, burnt orange and avocado over a cream ground — colours borrowed from sunshine, ceramics and walnut wood.

02

Clean geometric line

Thin confident outlines, flat graphic shapes and atomic motifs — starbursts and boomerangs drawn with a steady hand.

03

Form follows function

Organic curves where the hand falls, restraint everywhere else. Nothing decorative that is not also useful.
