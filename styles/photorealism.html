A UI style where every surface is rendered as if photographed — believable materials, true studio lighting, soft directional shadows, specular highlights, and grain. Less about icons of objects and more about photographs of them.

Skeuomorphism borrows the shape of a real object; Photorealism borrows its photograph. Where skeuomorphism is cartoon leather, Photorealism is leather grain shot under a softbox. The intent is verisimilitude, not metaphor.

Hi-fi product showcases, luxury commerce, music tools, watch and camera apps — anywhere the feeling of material warmth, weight, and craft adds to the perceived value of the thing on screen.

The Three Lights

What makes a UI photographable

Three principles, lifted from product photography — not metaphor, but technique.

Light 01 · Key

A real light source

Every surface on this page is lit from the upper left at roughly 3200 Kelvin. Highlights, shadows and fall-off all agree on where the lamp is.

Light 02 · Material

A believable substance

Aluminium scatters light in fine vertical streaks. Walnut shows its grain. Leather pebbles. Glass refracts. The pixels know what the thing is made of.

Light 03 · Grain

A trace of the camera

A fine layer of film grain, a soft vignette, and a touch of bokeh in the background sell the lie that the screen is, momentarily, a print.
