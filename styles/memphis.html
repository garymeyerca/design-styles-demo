An Italian design movement founded in Milan in 1981 by Ettore Sottsass. It threw out tasteful modernism in favour of clashing colour, geometric chaos, squiggles, terrazzo and a deliberately unserious attitude.

It can be — and this page proves it. Fat 3px black outlines give every control huge contrast, focus rings are loud dashed boxes, and text stays bold and legible despite all the confetti.

Brands with personality — music apps, festivals, kids' products, creative tools. Anywhere a little chaos signals fun, energy and a refusal to be boring.

Three rules, zero chill

Memphis is maximalism — joy from clash, not calm.

Rule 01

Clash on purpose

Hot pink next to cyan next to yellow. Harmony is banned — the friction between colours is the whole point.

Rule 02

Outline everything

Fat 3px black borders and hard offset shadows with zero blur. Shapes look like stickers slapped onto the page.

Rule 03

Scatter & tilt

Squiggles, dots, zigzags and terrazzo everywhere. Tilt panels a few degrees — perfect alignment is for cowards.
