FAQ

It gives interface elements human-like traits — eyes, smiles, little arms and legs. Controls stop being abstract widgets and become characters you can build a rapport with.

Faces are the first thing our brains look for. A control that reacts — a wink, a bounce, a grin — feels approachable, lowers anxiety, and makes the moment of using it genuinely delightful.

Playful products, kids' apps, onboarding flows and brand mascots — anywhere warmth beats clinical minimalism and a smile makes someone want to stick around.

Three traits, one happy crew

Anthropomorphic design is all about character.

Trait 01

Give it a face

Eyes and a mouth turn a widget into a personality. Suddenly people aren't using a control — they're meeting a friend.

Trait 02

Let it react

Bouncy, springy, expressive motion. Things wink, wobble and bob — every interaction earns a little reaction.

Trait 03

Keep it soft

Blobby, rounded, huggable shapes and warm colours. Nothing sharp, nothing cold — only charm.
