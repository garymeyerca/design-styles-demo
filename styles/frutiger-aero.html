FAQ

An aesthetic of the late 2000s — roughly 2004 to 2013 — defined by glossy glass, water droplets, bubbles, bright blue skies and lush green nature. Think Windows Vista, Web 2.0, and clean-tech optimism about a friendly digital future.

Skeuomorphism made digital surfaces feel tangible and aspirational. Specular highlights, beveled edges and vivid gradients suggested clean, polished, futuristic hardware — interfaces you would want to reach out and touch.

Upbeat, friendly products — media players, eco-tech dashboards, consumer apps — anywhere a fresh, glossy, optimistic mood beats cold minimalism. It is warm, wet and aspirational by nature.

Three rules, one bright feeling

Frutiger Aero is optimism — depth, gloss, and a clear blue future.

Rule 01

Wet glass & gloss

Every surface is a polished pane — a bright specular highlight up top, a vivid gradient below. Beveled, reflective, and freshly cleaned.

Rule 02

Sky & nature

Clear blue skies, lush green grass, water droplets and bubbles. Technology and nature, in optimistic harmony — eco-tech sheen.

Rule 03

Bright optimism

Vivid, saturated, aspirational. Soft bokeh, glassy reflections and a clean future that feels just one update away.
