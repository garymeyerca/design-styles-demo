The Catechism

An aesthetic of solemn grandeur — pointed arches, deep shadow, tarnished gilt and jewelled light. It draws everything upward, ornamenting every edge, treating the interface as a cathedral.

It can be. Parchment-toned text on near-black stone holds strong contrast, while gold accents, focus rings and clear labels keep every relic readable and operable in the gloom.

Atmosphere over efficiency — game lore, music archives, narrative sites and brand experiences where mood, mystery and craft matter more than brisk utility.

The Doctrine

Three Tenets, One Solemn Mood

Gothic design is devotion — ornament wrought toward the heavens.

Tenet
I

The Pointed Arch

Every form aspires upward. Arches, spires and pointed crowns draw the eye toward the vault — nothing rests flat or earthbound.

Tenet
II

Shadow & Jewel

Near-black stone holds the field. Light arrives only as tarnished gold and small panes of ruby, sapphire and emerald glass.

Tenet
III

Ornament Everywhere

Filigree, quatrefoils, hairline gold rules and tracery. No edge is left bare — the craft itself is the devotion.
