FAQ

An extreme form of chiaroscuro: violent, dramatic contrast between a single light source and an overwhelming, near-total darkness. Caravaggio made it a movement — figures struck by a shaft of light against a void.

Only with discipline. Anything that matters is pulled fully into the light — high-contrast amber on parchment — while everything secondary recedes. Focus rings and clear labels keep every control operable in the gloom.

The Painter's Method

Three rules of the single flame

Tenebrism is subtraction — let the dark do most of the work.

Rule 01

One source of light

A single warm flame, raking in from one direction. Never two. The drama lives entirely in what that one light chooses to reveal.

Rule 02

Let the dark dominate

The void is the canvas. Most of the page sits in deep umber shadow — only what truly matters is pulled forward into the gold.

Rule 03

Painterly, not printed

Soft gradients, warm bloom, classical serif. Edges dissolve rather than cut — every surface feels modelled in oil, never stamped on.
