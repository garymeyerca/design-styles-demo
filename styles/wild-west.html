Around the Campfire

A frontier aesthetic built on weathered kraft and parchment, saloon woodgrain, ornate woodtype straight off a WANTED poster, sheriff stars, rope borders, and the leather-and-brass hardware of the American Old West.

The big slab woodtype shines on headlines and labels. For body copy this page leans on a sturdy slab serif and a typewriter face, plus high-contrast oxblood ink, dashed focus rings, and honest labels — readable as a saloon menu.

Anywhere that wants rugged character — event posters, distilleries and breweries, games, festivals, and brands that trade on grit, craft, and a tall tale told well.

Three rules of the trail

The Wild West runs on grit — character carved from honest materials.

01

Honest materials

Weathered kraft, sawn oak plank, hammered brass and tanned leather. Nothin' slick — every surface looks worked by hand.

02

Woodtype hollers loud

Big tuscan and slab display fonts, stacked drop-shadows, sunburst rays. Headlines holler like a WANTED poster nailed to the saloon.

03

Sun-baked palette

Tan, rust, oxblood, sagebrush and sun-bleached sky. Hard wood edges, rope trim and brass tacks — the dust of the frontier.
