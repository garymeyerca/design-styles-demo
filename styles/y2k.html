FAQ

The bright, optimistic digital aesthetic of around 1998–2004 — glossy “Aqua” buttons, chrome gradients, holographic iridescence and a future that felt impossibly shiny.

New millennium hardware finally rendered smooth gradients and reflections, so designers leaned all the way in — every surface looked like polished plastic, metal or liquid glass.

Playful, loud, nostalgic products — music apps, launch pages, retro-tech brands — anywhere a burst of unironic early-2000s optimism is the point.
