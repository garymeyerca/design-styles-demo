Enquiries

A style born at the 1925 Paris Exposition — opulent and geometric, built on bilateral symmetry, sunbursts, chevrons and stepped ziggurats, all detailed in gleaming gold linework against deep, lacquered colour.

It can be. This salon pairs its decoration with high-contrast ivory text on emerald, visible focus rings, generous hit targets and clear labels — so every ornament stays legible and operable.

Where glamour is the point — luxury brands, hospitality, premium media and ceremonial interfaces that wish to feel composed, monied and unmistakably crafted.

The Doctrine

Three Rules, One Era

Art Deco is discipline dressed in gold.

I
Rule One

Strict Symmetry

Every composition balances on a central axis. Mirror the left in the right; let nothing list or wander.

II
Rule Two

Gold On Emerald

A deep, lacquered ground; thin precise gold linework. Restraint in colour, extravagance in detail.

III
Rule Three

Geometric Motifs

Sunbursts, chevrons, ziggurats and diamonds — machined ornament, repeated with measured precision.
