[M]FAQAccordioN

A design discipline rooted in the Swiss / International Typographic Style — every element snaps to a strict baseline and column grid, hierarchy comes from dramatic scale contrast, and typography itself does the structural work.

Restraint is the point. A monochrome base with a single signal colour keeps attention precise — the accent marks what matters, never decorates. Add a second colour and the system loses its voice.

Editorial interfaces, data-dense dashboards, posters and reference systems — anywhere clarity, order and a confident type voice outrank decoration.

Three Rules, One System

SEC.04

Modular typography is restraint — order from grid, voice from scale.

01

The Grid Is Visible

Columns and baselines are not hidden scaffolding — they are the page. Every edge aligns; structure is honestly on display.

02

Scale Carries Hierarchy

No ornament, no shadow — only dramatic contrast in type size. The biggest thing is the most important thing, every time.

03

Two Faces, One Accent

A powerful grotesk for display, a monospace for data labels, and one signal colour. The system stays disciplined and legible.
