Enquiries

A turn-of-the-century style that drew its forms straight from nature — flowing vines, stylised blossoms and the famous “whiplash” curve — uniting fine art and decorative craft into one ornamental whole.

The movement rejected the rigid straight line of industry. Its artists believed a sinuous, growing line — like a stem reaching for light — carried far more grace and life than any mechanical edge.

In posters, stained glass, ironwork and jewellery — anywhere a frame can be drawn by hand and a flourish can earn its place. Here it lends warmth and craft to an otherwise ordinary control panel.

Three guiding lines

Art Nouveau is nature, drawn — ornament with intention.

     
I

The whiplash line

Every contour curves and recoils like a tendril in the wind. The straight line is banished; energy lives in the bend.

     
II

Botany & ornament

Flowers, vines, peacock plumes and stylised leaves frame the work. Nature is the pattern-book; decoration is never apology.

     
III

The drawn frame

Each panel is enclosed by a hand-rendered border — gilt, inked and cornered with curls. The frame is part of the picture.
