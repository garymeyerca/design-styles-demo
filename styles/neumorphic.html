FAQ

A UI style where elements share the background colour and gain depth purely from soft inner and outer shadows — as if shapes were pressed into, or extruded from, a sheet of clay.

On its own, not very — low contrast is its core weakness. This page pairs it with solid accent colours, visible focus rings and clear labels to keep controls legible and operable.

Three rules, one feeling

Neumorphism is restraint — depth from light alone.

Rule 01

One surface tone

Elements and background share the exact same colour. Contrast is forbidden — shape emerges only from shadow.

Rule 02

Two opposing shadows

A dark shadow below-right, a light highlight above-left. Extruded surfaces cast out; pressed ones cast inset.

Rule 03

Soft, generous radii

Rounded corners and blurred shadows keep everything calm and tactile — like pebbles, never paper cuts.
