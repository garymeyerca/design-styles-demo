An ornamental metalwork tradition where fine threads of gold or silver are twisted and soldered into delicate, lace-like scrollwork — here translated into hairline borders, curling corners and gilded detail.

Not when it is disciplined. The decoration stays at hairline weight and is confined to frames and dividers, while content sits on calm ink with clear focus rings and generous contrast.

Three Rules of the Craft

Filigree is patience — beauty drawn in the finest thread.

Rule the First

Hairline weight

Every line is drawn at a single thread's thickness. Ornament earns its place only when it stays delicate enough to never shout.

Rule the Second

Curl the corners

Frames close with scrollwork curls and lace. The edge of a thing is where the craft is shown — never leave a corner bare.

Rule the Third

Gold upon ink

Antique gold set against deep ink lets each gilded line glow. Darkness is the velvet on which the jewel is laid.
