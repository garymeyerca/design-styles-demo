FAQ

A layout style where content lives in rounded modular blocks of varied sizes, tiled tightly like a Japanese bento tray. The grid itself carries the design — even gaps, soft shadows, crisp labels.

It scans fast and scales well. Each block is self-contained, so you can reorder, resize and reuse them freely — perfect for SaaS feature pages and Apple-style keynote layouts.

Overview screens — dashboards, landing pages, settings panels — anywhere a lot of distinct pieces need to feel organised, premium and calm rather than dense or noisy.

Three rules, one tray

Bento design is order — varied blocks held together by rhythm.

Section 03
Rule 01

Modular blocks

Each idea gets its own rounded tile. Varied sizes — wide, tall, square — packed into one consistent grid.

Rule 02

Even gaps

One consistent gutter between every block. The rhythm of the negative space is what makes the tray feel tidy.

Rule 03

Calm restraint

Neutral palette, one subtle accent, soft shadows, crisp icons-with-labels. Premium-minimal — never loud.
