FAQ

A UI style where panels behave like frosted glass — a semi-transparent fill, a backdrop blur that scatters whatever sits behind them, a hairline light border, and a soft drop shadow. Depth comes from layering, not from solid colour.

It can be — the risk is text contrast over a busy background. This page pairs the blur with near-white text, generous saturation, visible focus rings and solid accent fills so every control stays legible.

Anywhere a vivid backdrop adds life — dashboards, media players, OS-style overlays — where translucency makes interfaces feel light, layered and modern.

Three rules, one feeling

Glassmorphism is depth through transparency — light, fresh, layered.

Rule 01

Translucent fill

Surfaces are a faint white wash — 10–20% opacity — so the colourful field behind reads softly through every panel.

Rule 02

Backdrop blur

A 16–22px blur frosts whatever sits behind. It is the glass — without it translucency just looks faded.

Rule 03

Hairline & shadow

A 1px bright border catches the light along the top edge, and a soft diffuse shadow lifts the pane off the page.
