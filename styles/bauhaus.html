FAQ

A modernist design language from the 1919 Bauhaus school: a strict primary palette, the circle, square and triangle as a complete visual vocabulary, a visible structural grid, and the conviction that form follows function.

The three primaries cannot be mixed from other colours — they are the irreducible base. Limiting the palette to red, blue, yellow plus black and white forces clarity: colour becomes signal, never decoration.

Posters, signage, editorial layouts and functional interfaces — anywhere a rigorous grid and bold, legible geometry communicate faster than ornament.

three rules, one system

Bauhaus is discipline — geometry, primaries, and the grid.

01

primary palette

Red, blue, yellow — plus black and white. Nothing else. Each colour carries meaning; the eye is never asked to decode a gradient.

02

pure geometry

The circle, the square, the triangle. Every component is assembled from these three forms — a complete vocabulary with no exceptions.

03

the visible grid

Structure is honest. Hard rules divide every region; alignment is the composition. Form follows function — decoration follows nothing.
